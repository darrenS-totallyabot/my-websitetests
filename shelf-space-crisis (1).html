HOW THIS ALL STARTED

Real talk — I started this because I genuinely couldn't stop buying figures and cars, and my room looks like a museum that ran out of budget for proper shelving. Hence the name. But the story goes back way further than that.

I grew up in the Philippines, and if you know, you know — the toys out there are cool but they hit different when you compare them to what was coming out of Japan and the US. My neighbors had the newest stuff. Like actually new stuff. And I remember being a kid just thinking "man I wish I had that." I did get toys — don't get me wrong, my family made sure birthdays and Christmas hit — but it wasn't like my neighbor who seemed to get something new every other week. That kind of stuck with me.

"Growing up, toys felt like a privilege. Now that I can buy them myself, I'm making up for lost time — and yeah, my shelf situation is definitely paying the price."

Fast forward to me finally being able to work and earn my own money. The first figure I ever bought with my own cash was the S.H.Figuarts Gojo Satoru — the original, from back when it first dropped. I remember holding it and thinking this was the coolest thing I'd ever owned. That was it. That was the moment I was gone. No going back.

THE FIGURE SIDE

Since that first Gojo, I've been deep in the figure game. SHF, Nendoroids, prize figures, scale figures — I'm not picky as long as it looks incredible on a shelf. I genuinely research what I buy. I'm not just flipping stuff, I'm a fan first. Every figure I sell is something I'd either own already or put on my own shelf without hesitation.

I know what bad quality looks like, I know what the good manufacturers are, and I'm not about wasting your money on something that's going to look rough after two months. If it's in the store, it's passed my own standard first.

The beginning
First SHF Gojo. The one that started everything. Sat on my desk and I just stared at it for like 20 minutes.
The deep end
Started collecting more seriously. Learned the difference between scale figures, prize figures, and the premium stuff. Opinions were formed. Money disappeared.
Shelf Space Crisis
The name came naturally. My room is literally a crisis. Started the Instagram, started selling some pieces, and here we are.

THE CAR SIDE

Parallel to the figure obsession is the car obsession. Specifically 90s JDM. Skylines, Supras, AE86s, NSXs, RX-7s — the era before everything got too perfect and digital. Cars that had character, that you could hear, that meant something.

I collect 1:64 scale diecasts of these cars because, well, the real things are either impossible to find or astronomically priced. But the little ones? You can have a whole fleet on one shelf. They're detailed, they're satisfying, and they let you appreciate the design in a way that you can actually hold in your hand.

The cars section here is an extension of that love. Same philosophy as the figures — I wouldn't sell anything I wouldn't keep myself.

WHY BUY FROM HERE?

Honestly? Because I'm you. I'm not a big corporation that just sources whatever sells. I'm a 22-year-old who grew up wishing he had the toys his neighbors had, who finally got to start his own collection, and who wants to share that joy with other people who get it.

The giveaways exist because I want the community around this page to feel like something real — not just another resell account. Stick around on Instagram, interact, show up — and you'll get looked after. That's the vibe here.

"This isn't just a store. It's my hobby turned into something I can share. Come for the figures and cars, stay for the community."
